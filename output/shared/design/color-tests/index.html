Evaluation Criteria

Criteria Scheme A: Mono-Violet Scheme B: Dark Terminal Scheme C: Warm Neutral

Hierarchy Clarity Good - Purple pops on white Excellent - Blue glows stand out Excellent - Orange is unmissable

Accessibility Good - 4.5:1 contrast ratios Excellent - High contrast Good - Warm tones accessible

Scheme A Recommendation

Best choice if you want to appeal to a broad audience including non-technical founders. Clean, professional, works for both light and dark preferences.

Best For Broad appeal, professional services, enterprise-leaning

Scheme B Recommendation

Best choice if your primary audience is developers and technical users. The terminal aesthetic resonates with the "export-first" philosophy.

Best For Developer tools, CLI-focused, technical audience

Scheme C Recommendation

Best choice if you want to stand out from the purple/blue developer tool crowd. Orange is bold, memorable, and suggests energy and action.

Best For Differentiation, startup energy, premium positioning
